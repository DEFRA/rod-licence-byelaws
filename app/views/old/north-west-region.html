1. Overview of North West Byelaws

These byelaws for the North West Region apply to anyone fishing with a rod and line for salmon, trout, coarse fish, eels, smelt and lamprey in all freshwater fisheries.

Private fisheries may enforce their own additional rules, but national and regional byelaws still apply.

2. When and where you can fish

National close season rules for coarse fish apply across the North West Region.

When can I fish for salmon and migratory trout?

The annual close season for salmon: 1 November - 31 January, with the following exception:

The annual close season on the River Eden (including all tributaries and connected lakes and waters): 15 October - 14 January.

The annual close season for migratory trout: 1 October - 31st March.

Non-migratory trout - annual close season

The annual close season for non-migratory trout (not rainbow trout) in all waters (except fully enclosed stillwaters): 1 October - 14 March.

The annual close season for rainbow trout: 1 October - 14 March in all rivers, streams, drains and canals.

Char - annual close season

The annual close season for char: 1 October - 14 March, except in the following waters:

  • Coniston Water: 1 November - 30 April
  • Lake Windermere: 1 October - 30 April
  • Lake Windermere: you may fish for char from 15 March from a moving boat using artificial lures.

Fishing near weirs and other obstructions

No fishing is allowed within a distance of 20 metres above and 50 metres below the crest of the following weirs:

  • Branthwaite Weir, River Marron
  • Calder Hall Pumphouse Weir, River Calder, West Cumbria
  • Stramongate Weir, River Kent
  • Holmehead Weir, River Caldew
  • Buckabank Weir, River Caldew

No fishing is allowed within a distance of 20 metres above and 75 metres below the crest of the following weirs

  • Ennerdale Mill Weir, River Ehen

Fish passes

Fishing in a fish pass or within a 5 metre radius of the entrance and exit of any fish pass in not allowed.

Skerton Weir

Fishing on the River Lune is not allowed between the crest of Skerton Weir (including the fish pass) and the four black and white marker posts placed to create a line across the river between national OS grid references: SD48036307, SD48106311, SD48166320 and SD48276320. See map below:

Between the line formed by the four black and white marker posts and the line formed by the three red and white marker posts placed down river (on national OS grid references SD48126292, SD48186298 and SD48256296) only fly fishing with rod and line is permitted, provided that no weighted device is attached to the line, cast or hook.

Beyond the red markers, national rules apply.

Ribble Catchment

Between 30 September and the start of the salmon close season (1 Nov), fishing is not allowed within a distance of 20 metres above and 50 metres below the crest of any man-made construction impounding water on the River Ribble and its tributaries:

  • upstream of the Naze at Freckleton (excluding Rivers Darwen, Calder and Douglas)
  • and in all rivers and streams north of the Ribble catchment.

River Border Esk

The Upper Esk, commonly known as the 'Border Esk' marks the border between England and Scotland. However, fishing on either bank of the Esk is subject to the national and NW regional byelaws.

Byelaws for the Border Esk and its tributaries (incl. the River Sark) are listed below and in the section called 'Catch limits, size limits, catch returns'.

  • Annual close season for salmon: 1 Nov - 31 Jan
  • It is prohibited to catch and take salmon alive or dead before 16 June.
  • Annual close season for migratory trout: 1 Oct - 31 Mar
  • Annual close season for non-migratory trout (not incl. rainbow trout): 1 Oct - 14 Mar
  • Annual close season for freshwater fish and eels - national byelaws apply

Fishing for eels

The annual close season for eels in non-tidal parts of rivers, streams and those parts of rivers canalised for navigation purposes: 15 March - 15 June.

There is no close season in tidal waters.

Waters where no fishing is allowed

There is a prohibition of all types of fishing in certain waters in Kendal.

On the River Kent between Victoria Bridge and its confluence with the former Dockray Hall mill race, situated 400 metres upstream of Victoria Bridge in the Borough of Kendal - fishing from the right (westerly) bank is prohibited.

This rule does not apply to fly fishing so long as no weights are attached or incorporated into the line, cast or hook.

3. Legal fishing tackle, lures and bait

Bait

There are regional rules on the use of baits on the following named lakes:

Windermere, Coniston, Wastwater, Ennerdale Water, Crummock Water, Buttermere, Loweswater, Bassenthwaite, Derwentwater, Brotherswater, Ullswater, Red Tarn, Haweswater and Thirlmere.

These rules are:

  • The use of any dead or alive freshwater fish, salmonids or eels as bait is prohibited.
  • Possession of any live freshwater fish, salmonids or eels with the intention of using them as bait, ground bait, loose feed is prohibited.

From 15 March - 15 June on lakes Windermere, Coniston, Wastwater, Ennerdale Water, Crummock Water, Buttermere, Loweswater, Bassenthwaite, Derwentwater, Brotherswater, Ullswater, Rydalwater and Grasmere:

  • Fishing for salmon, migratory trout, non-migratory trout, eel and shad with maggots, their pupae or imitations thereof is prohibited.
  • The use of the baits specified in above as lures or baits not on or attached to a hook is prohibited.

From 15 March - 15 June in rivers, streams and those parts of rivers canalised for navigation:

  • Fishing for Salmon and Migratory Trout will only will be allowed with minnow, worm, shrimp, prawn or artificial flies or lures
  • The use of any lure or bait not on or attached to a hook is prohibited.

Using lures

There are some restrictions on the weight of lures, and the size of hooks attached to lures.

After the 31st day of August, when fishing for salmon and migratory trout:

  • A lure with double, treble or multiple hooks attached - the gape of each hook should not be more than 8mm and the weight of the lure should be no more than 15g.
  • A lure with a single hook - the weight of the lure should be no more than 30g.

These rules do not apply to the following rivers, river lakes and tributaries:

  • River Eden, Derwent, Ellen, Waver, Wampool, Annas, Bleng, Esk, Mite, Irt, Calder and Ehen
  • To fishing for char in lakes Windermere, Ennerdale, Coniston, Rydal Water and Grasmere.

Lead weights

You must not use any form of lead weight attached to a fishing line other than those of 0.06 grams (size No. 8), or less, or of more than 28.35 grams (1 ounce).

"Lead weight" does not include lead incorporated into a weighted line, swim-feeder or fishing fly or lure.

Worms and hooks

When fishing for Salmon or Trout using worms as bait, the following rules on the number of hooks and hook size apply:

  • When using a single hook attached to a line, the hook must be no more than 40mm in length and with a gape of no more than 15mm.
  • When using two or three hooks attached to a line, hooks must be no more than 15mm in length and with a gape of no more than 8mm.
  • No more than three hooks may be attached to a single line.
  • The total weight of any lead weights used to sink the line must not exceed 30g.

Rods and nets

Rules around the number of rods permitted, use of landing nets, keepnets, gaffs or tailers are all covered by National Byelaws with the following exceptions:

Keep nets

On rivers, streams, canalised rivers and on Lake Windermere, Coniston, Rydal Water, Grasmere, Ullswater, Brothers Water, Bassenthwaite, Derwentwater, Loweswater, Crummock Water, Buttermere, Ennerdale Water and Wastwater, the use of a keep net is prohibited during the statutory close season for coarse fish.

4. Catch limits, size limits, catch returns

National Byelaws on catch limits and size limits apply across the North West region with the following exceptions:

  • Migratory Trout size limit - must be not less than 30cm
  • Brown Trout and Char size limit - must be not less than 20cm

Additional size limits enforced on the Border Esk:

  • Chub must be not less than 25cm
  • Dace must be not less than 20cm
  • Grayling must be not less than 23cm

The size shall be ascertained by measuring from the tip of the snout to the fork or cleft of the tail.

Catch return reporting

Anyone holding a salmon and migratory trout rod licence is legally obliged to complete an annual catch return report, as outlined in the National Byelaws. Find out how to complete a catch return online.
